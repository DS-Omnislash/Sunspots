================================================================================
MODEL IMPROVEMENTS WORTH TRYING
================================================================================

--- HIGH IMPACT, LOW EFFORT ---

[x] 5. Prediction intervals via quantile regression.
        DONE — notebooks/03-Optimization.ipynb. Trains lower (α=0.10), median (α=0.50),
        and upper (α=0.90) LightGBM quantile models in the same walk-forward framework.
        Result: coverage 67.3% (target 80%), median RMSE 33.91 — intervals work but
        standalone LGB drops the hybrid architecture advantage. Proper solution would
        be conformal prediction (MAPIE) wrapping the full hybrid.

[-] 5b. Optuna hyperparameter tuning (50 trials, TPE sampler).
        TRIED — best walk-forward RMSE 19.83 vs baseline 19.24 (3.1% worse).
        Fixed 2-year holdout biased optimisation toward Solar Cycle 25 peak params.
        Model appears near its ceiling for this feature set.

--- MEDIUM EFFORT, INTERESTING RESULTS ---

[ ] 6. Blend ARIMA into the hybrid.
        The horizon sweep showed ARIMA is competitive at longer horizons.
        A simple weighted average of Hybrid + ARIMA predictions, with weights
        that shift toward ARIMA as horizon grows, could improve the 10-30 day range.

[ ] 7. Multi-output forecasting.
        Currently run_future_forecast trains a fresh model per step. Training one
        model with a multi-output target [t+1, t+2, ..., t+5] using
        MultiOutputRegressor would be faster and might capture cross-horizon structure.

--- HIGHER EFFORT, HIGHER CEILING ---

[ ] 8. N-BEATS / N-HiTS (neuralforecast library).
        Neural architectures built specifically for time series — no manual feature
        engineering, handle multiple horizons natively, regularly top leaderboards
        on solar/energy forecasting benchmarks. Would make for a direct
        "classical ML vs deep learning" comparison.

[-] 9. Exogenous variable: F10.7 solar flux index.
        TRIED — RMSE 19.90 vs baseline 19.24 (marginally worse). F10.7 is too
        correlated with existing sunspot lag features to add independent signal.
        Infrastructure kept in src/data.py (load_solar_flux) and src/features.py
        (conditional F10.7 features) but not active in the main pipeline.
